\title{Polarized CMB Foregrounds with Machine Learning and Fluid Simulations}
\author{David Collins \altaffilmark{1}, Kevin M. Huffenberger
\altaffilmark{2}, perhaps others}

\altaffiltext{1}{Department of Physics, Florida State University}
\altaffiltext{2}{Mitchell Institute for Fundamental Physics \& Astronomy and
Department of Physics \& Astronomy,
Texas A\&M University, College Station, Texas 77843, U.S.A.}


\begin{abstract}
    We developed a machine learning model to remove polarized foregrounds from
    future measurements of the CMB.  It uses a convolutional neural network to
    produce maps of $E$ and $B$ from temperature map $T$ alone.  The novelty of this model is that it has
    been trained on hydrodynamical simulations, so naturally accounts for
    correlations between density and magnetic field that directly impact $Q$ and
    $U$.  \textbf{We can extract a background signal from a noisy signal, that's a
    lie but we need to.}
\end{abstract}
